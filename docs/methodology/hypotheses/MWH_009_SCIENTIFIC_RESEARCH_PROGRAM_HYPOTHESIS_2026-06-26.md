# MWH-009 – Scientific Research Program Hypothesis

---

# 1. Objective

The objective of this working hypothesis is to investigate whether large scientific capability development programs eventually require an additional organizational layer dedicated to managing scientific research questions.

The hypothesis concerns scientific knowledge organization.

It does not propose changes to the Scientific Derivation Methodology itself.

---

# 2. Motivation

Completion of multiple capability derivations has produced two different categories of scientific knowledge.

The first category consists of validated scientific knowledge, including:

* Scientific Capabilities,
* Scientific Findings,
* Methodology,
* Governance.

The second category consists of unresolved scientific knowledge, including:

* open scientific questions,
* unresolved methodological questions,
* partially supported hypotheses,
* candidate future investigations.

At present these research-oriented artifacts remain distributed across multiple documents.

---

# 3. Current Observation

After completion of SGF-011 and SGF-012 the number of documented open scientific questions has increased.

These questions originate from different sources, including:

* capability derivations,
* methodology reviews,
* roadmap reviews,
* architecture reviews.

The observations indicate increasing scientific research activity.

The long-term organizational implications remain unknown.

---

# 4. Working Hypothesis

Hypothesis

A sufficiently large Scientific Representation Platform may eventually benefit from an explicit scientific research management layer responsible for organizing unresolved scientific questions independently from validated scientific knowledge.

The proposed layer would organize research activities.

It would not replace:

* Capability Specifications,
* Scientific Findings,
* Governance,
* Methodology.

---

# 5. Scientific Motivation

Scientific knowledge and scientific research represent different stages of scientific development.

Validated scientific knowledge describes conclusions supported by sufficient evidence.

Scientific research describes questions that remain unresolved.

Maintaining both categories separately may improve long-term knowledge organization as the platform expands.

This proposition remains unvalidated.

---

# 6. Expected Responsibilities

If such a research layer eventually becomes justified, potential responsibilities may include:

* organization of open scientific questions,
* prioritization of future investigations,
* tracking of unresolved methodological questions,
* documentation of supporting evidence,
* documentation of research progress,
* traceability between research questions and completed capability derivations.

These responsibilities are conceptual only.

They are not currently adopted.

---

# 7. Scientific Questions

The following questions require investigation.

Does the number of unresolved scientific questions continue to increase across future capability derivations?

Can existing governance documents adequately organize these questions?

Does a dedicated research organization layer improve scientific traceability?

Does such a layer reduce duplication of research planning?

Can similar benefits be achieved through existing documentation structures?

No answers are currently available.

---

# 8. Required Evidence

The following evidence would strengthen or weaken this hypothesis.

Evidence supporting the hypothesis may include:

* repeated accumulation of open scientific questions,
* increasing difficulty locating research topics,
* repeated cross-capability research dependencies,
* governance reviews identifying organizational limitations.

Evidence against the hypothesis may include:

* existing documentation remaining sufficient despite continued platform growth,
* absence of measurable organizational benefits,
* successful management of research questions using existing governance structures.

---

# 9. Current Assessment

Current evidence consists of observations from two completed capability derivations.

This evidence is insufficient to justify architectural modification.

The hypothesis therefore remains exploratory.

No implementation is recommended.

---

# 10. Validation Strategy

The hypothesis shall be re-evaluated after multiple additional capability derivations.

Suggested evaluation points include:

* SGF-015
* SGF-020
* future Scientific Knowledge Architecture Reviews

These milestones are recommendations for future evaluation rather than mandatory review points.

---

# 11. Methodological Status

Current classification

Methodological Working Hypothesis.

Scientific confidence

Low.

Validation status

Insufficient evidence.

Architectural impact

None.

Methodological impact

None.

No change to the Scientific Derivation Methodology or Scientific Knowledge Architecture is justified at the present time.

---

# 12. Scientific Conclusion

Current observations suggest that long-term scientific capability development may eventually require additional organization of unresolved research questions.

The available evidence is presently insufficient to justify introducing a new architectural layer.

The hypothesis shall therefore remain a Methodological Working Hypothesis until additional capability derivations provide sufficient evidence for scientific evaluation.

Future Methodology Reviews and Scientific Knowledge Architecture Reviews should reassess this hypothesis using accumulated empirical evidence rather than isolated observations.
