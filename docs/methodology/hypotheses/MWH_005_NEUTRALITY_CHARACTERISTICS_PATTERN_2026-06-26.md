# Objective

Document the observation that neutrality characteristics have appeared repeatedly during multiple Scientific Capability pilots.

This document intentionally does not introduce any methodological standard.

---

# Background

During multiple Scientific Capability derivations it was observed that several characteristics do not describe what a capability represents.

Instead, they explicitly describe what the capability intentionally does not represent.

These neutrality characteristics appear to serve as scientific boundary protection.

---

# Current Evidence

- Structural Neutrality

## SGF-009

- Dynamic Neutrality
- Causal Neutrality

---

# Observation

The identified neutrality characteristics share several common properties.

They do not introduce new scientific capability.

Instead they explicitly restrict scientific responsibility.

They protect capability boundaries.

They prevent responsibility overlap between neighbouring scientific capabilities.

They support preservation of the Single Scientific Responsibility Principle.

---

# Current Assessment

Current evidence consists of two independent Scientific Capability pilots.

This is sufficient to recognise a possible methodological pattern.

It is not sufficient to conclude that neutrality characteristics are mandatory for every future capability.

Therefore no methodological standardization is justified.

---

# Future Evaluation Criteria

This working hypothesis should be re-evaluated after additional capability pilots.

Evidence should be collected regarding:

- recurrence frequency,

- contribution to capability boundary definition.

Only repeated observations should justify methodological evolution.

# Scientific Finding

Multiple capability pilots independently produced neutrality characteristics.

Current evidence suggests a recurring methodological pattern.

The available evidence remains insufficient for methodological standardization.
