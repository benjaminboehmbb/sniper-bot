# SGF-013C – Capability Observation Collection

---

# 1. Objective

The objective of this phase is to collect scientific observations relevant to the candidate capability Scientific Behaviour Representation.

Only observations are collected.

No interpretation, classification or property derivation is performed during this phase.

---

# 2. Observation Collection Principles

Collected observations shall satisfy the following criteria.

* observable,
* implementation independent,
* domain independent,
* reproducible,
* scientifically distinguishable.

Observations shall describe only what can be observed.

---

# 3. Observation O-001

Scientific evolution may exhibit different behavioural characteristics while preserving identical structural representation.

Observation

Structural representation alone does not completely describe behavioural characteristics.

---

# 4. Observation O-002

Scientific evolution may exhibit identical temporal properties while differing in behavioural characteristics.

Observation

Temporal representation alone does not uniquely determine behaviour.

# 5. Observation O-003

Observation

Dynamic characterization alone does not completely represent behaviour.

# 6. Observation O-004

Observation

Behaviour is associated with evolution rather than isolated scientific entities.

Observation

# 9. Observation O-007

Observation

# 10. Observation O-008

Behavioural characteristics may remain recognizable under different structural realizations.

Observation

Behaviour is not restricted to one particular structural configuration.

# 11. Observation O-009

Behavioural characteristics may describe persistent patterns of scientific evolution.

Observation

Behaviour appears to characterize evolution across multiple observations rather than isolated events.

# 12. Observation O-010

Observation

Behavioural representation appears to depend upon previously represented scientific information without replacing it.

# 13. Observation Summary

The collected observations indicate that behavioural characteristics constitute an observable aspect of scientific evolution that is distinguishable from:

* structural identity,
* structural ordering,
* transitions,
* trajectories,
* dynamics,
* temporal representation.

These observations support continued scientific evaluation.
