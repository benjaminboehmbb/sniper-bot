# SGF-012C – Capability Observation Collection

---

# 1. Objective

The objective of this phase is to collect scientific observations describing Scientific Time Representation.

The observations shall be independent of implementation, application domain and algorithmic realization.

At this stage no Necessary Properties are derived.

Only observable characteristics are collected.

---

# 2. Observation Collection Principles

Each observation shall satisfy the following criteria.

* scientifically observable,
* capability-specific,
* independent of implementation,
* reproducible,
* suitable for later validation.

Observations are intentionally descriptive rather than explanatory.

---

# 3. Observation O-001

Observation

Temporal properties may exist independently of behavioural interpretation.

Evidence

Temporal duration, temporal interval and temporal reference can be represented without describing behavioural evolution.

Interpretation

Temporal representation appears independent from behaviour.

---

# 4. Observation O-002

Observation

Temporal properties may characterize scientific structures without modifying those structures.

Evidence

A scientific state remains the same scientific state regardless of whether temporal information is attached.

Interpretation

Time acts as additional scientific representation rather than structural modification.

---

# 5. Observation O-003

Observation

Temporal properties are applicable to multiple scientific entities.

Evidence

Temporal information may be associated with:

* Scientific States,
* Scientific Relationships,
* Scientific Transitions,
* Scientific Trajectories.

Interpretation

Scientific Time Representation is not restricted to a single existing capability.

---

# 6. Observation O-004

Observation

Temporal representation preserves scientific neutrality.

Evidence

Temporal information alone does not imply behaviour, prediction or causality.

Interpretation

Temporal representation constitutes descriptive scientific information.

---

# 7. Observation O-005

Observation

Different temporal representations may correspond to the same structural representation.

Evidence

Identical trajectories may differ in duration, interval or temporal spacing while preserving identical structural order.

Interpretation

Structural representation and temporal representation are scientifically distinguishable.

---

# 8. Observation O-006

Observation

Temporal properties may exist without causal explanation.

Evidence

A temporal interval can be represented without explaining why the interval exists.

Interpretation

Temporal representation is independent from causal reasoning.

---

# 9. Observation O-007

Observation

Temporal properties may exist without predictive capability.

Evidence

Representing elapsed time does not require predicting future temporal evolution.

Interpretation

Temporal representation precedes forecasting.

---

# 10. Observation O-008

Observation

Temporal representation introduces additional scientific information.

Evidence

Without explicit temporal representation, duration and interval cannot become representable scientific concepts.

Interpretation

Scientific expressive power increases.

---

# 11. Observation O-009

Observation

Temporal properties are measurable independently of semantic interpretation.

Evidence

Temporal duration may be represented without assigning application-specific meaning.

Interpretation

Scientific Time Representation remains domain independent.

---

# 12. Observation O-010

Observation

Temporal representation preserves previously validated capability responsibilities.

Evidence

Scientific State, Relationship, Ordering, Transition, Trajectory and Dynamics retain their validated responsibilities after introducing temporal representation.

Interpretation

Scientific Time Representation extends rather than replaces the existing capability hierarchy.

---

# 13. Observation Summary

The collected observations consistently indicate that Scientific Time Representation:

* represents temporal properties,
* preserves structural representations,
* remains behaviourally neutral,
* remains causally neutral,
* remains prediction neutral,
* increases representational capability,
* preserves capability responsibilities.

No collected observation contradicts the conclusions of the previous investigation phases.
