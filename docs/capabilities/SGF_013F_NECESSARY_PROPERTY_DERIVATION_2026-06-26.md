# SGF-013F – Necessary Property Derivation

---

# 1. Objective

The objective of this phase is to derive the Necessary Properties of Scientific Behaviour Representation from the validated capability characteristics.

A Necessary Property is indispensable for fulfilling the scientific responsibility of the capability.

Removal of a Necessary Property would reduce or eliminate the capability's scientific responsibility.

---

# 2. Derivation Principle

Necessary Properties shall satisfy all of the following conditions.

* Directly derived from validated capability characteristics.
* Scientifically necessary.
* Capability-specific.
* Independent from implementation.
* Independent from application domain.

Only properties satisfying all conditions are accepted.

---

# 3. Derived Necessary Properties

## NP-001 — Behaviour Representation

Scientific Behaviour Representation shall explicitly represent behavioural characteristics of scientific evolution.

Scientific justification

Derived from C-001.

Without explicit behavioural representation the capability cannot fulfill its scientific responsibility.

---

## NP-002 — Evolution Association

Behavioural representation shall be associated with scientific evolution.

Scientific justification

Derived from C-002.

Behaviour cannot be represented independently of scientific evolution.

---

## NP-003 — Structural Independence

Behavioural representation shall remain distinguishable from structural representation.

Scientific justification

Derived from C-003.

Otherwise Behaviour Representation becomes reducible to existing structural capabilities.

---

## NP-004 — Temporal Independence

Behavioural representation shall remain distinguishable from temporal representation.

Scientific justification

Derived from C-004.

Otherwise Behaviour Representation becomes reducible to Scientific Time Representation.

---

## NP-005 — Dynamic Independence

Behavioural representation shall remain distinguishable from dynamic characterization.

Scientific justification

Derived from C-005.

Otherwise Behaviour Representation becomes reducible to Scientific Dynamics.

---

## NP-006 — Behaviour Persistence

Behavioural characteristics shall remain representable across multiple Scientific States whenever supported by the represented scientific information.

Scientific justification

Derived from C-006.

Without persistence Behaviour Representation would be limited to isolated observations rather than behavioural evolution.

---

## NP-007 — Hierarchical Integration

Behaviour Representation shall build upon previously validated scientific representations without modifying their scientific responsibilities.

Scientific justification

Derived from C-007.

This preserves architectural consistency and responsibility separation.

---

---

# 5. Necessary Property Summary

The derived Necessary Properties describe Behaviour Representation as a capability that:

* explicitly represents behavioural characteristics,
* is associated with scientific evolution,
* remains distinguishable from structural representation,
* remains distinguishable from temporal representation,
* remains distinguishable from dynamic characterization,
* supports behavioural persistence,
* integrates hierarchically with previously validated capabilities.
