# SGF-013B.3 – Capability Boundary Investigation

---

# 1. Objective

The objective of this investigation is to determine the scientific boundary of Scientific Behaviour Representation.

The investigation identifies which scientific responsibilities belong to the candidate capability and which remain assigned to previously validated or future capabilities.

A capability boundary is considered valid only if every scientific responsibility can be assigned unambiguously.

---

# 2. Boundary Investigation Principle

The boundary investigation evaluates whether the candidate capability possesses:

* a unique scientific responsibility,
* clear separation from neighbouring capabilities,
* no responsibility overlap,
* no unnecessary responsibility expansion.

The resulting boundary shall remain implementation independent and domain independent.

---

# 3. Lower Capability Boundary

Scientific Behaviour Representation is evaluated relative to previously validated capabilities.

Scientific State

Represents existence.

Scientific Relationship

Represents relationships.

Scientific Ordering

Represents ordering.

Scientific Transition

Represents structural change.

Scientific Trajectory

Represents structural evolution.

Scientific Dynamics

Represents characteristics of structural evolution.

Scientific Time Representation

Represents temporal properties.

None of these validated responsibilities explicitly represent behavioural characteristics.

Current evidence indicates that Behaviour Representation occupies a distinct responsibility.

---

# 4. Upper Capability Boundary

Scientific Behaviour Representation is evaluated relative to anticipated future capabilities.

Forecast Representation

Responsible for representing expected future evolution.

Scientific Causality Representation

Responsible for representing causal relationships.

Decision-related capabilities

Responsible for selecting actions or alternatives.

Scientific Behaviour Representation shall not assume these responsibilities.

---

# 5. Responsibility Boundary

The candidate responsibility is evaluated.

Candidate responsibility:

Representation of behavioural characteristics of scientific evolution.

The capability shall not:

* explain behaviour,
* predict behaviour,
* optimize behaviour,
* control behaviour,
* evaluate behaviour.

Its responsibility is limited to scientific representation.

---

# 6. Information Boundary

Scientific Behaviour Representation may use information produced by previous capabilities.

It does not modify:

* structural information,
* temporal information,
* trajectory information,
* dynamics information.

Instead, it represents an additional information class describing behavioural characteristics.

Current evidence supports information separation.

---

# 7. Capability Interaction

Interaction with existing capabilities is evaluated.

Scientific Behaviour Representation consumes previously represented scientific information.

It does not redefine or replace existing representations.

Future capabilities may subsequently consume Behaviour Representation.

The resulting interaction is hierarchical rather than overlapping.

---

# 8. Boundary Consistency Review

The proposed boundary is evaluated for consistency.

Responsibility overlap

Not identified.

Responsibility ambiguity

Not identified.

Architectural conflict

Not identified.

Capability separation

Supported by current evidence.

---

# 9. Scientific Assessment

Unique responsibility

Supported.

Boundary clarity

Supported.

Responsibility separation

Supported.

Architectural compatibility

Supported.

Further validation required during subsequent derivation phases.

---

# 10. Scientific Decision

Current evidence supports a distinct scientific boundary for Scientific Behaviour Representation.

The candidate capability maintains separation from previously validated and anticipated future capabilities according to the currently available evidence.

---

# 11. Scientific Conclusion

The boundary investigation indicates that Scientific Behaviour Representation can be assigned a distinct scientific responsibility while preserving the responsibility boundaries of neighbouring capabilities.
