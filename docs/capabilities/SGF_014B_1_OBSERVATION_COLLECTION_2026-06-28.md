# Scientific Capability

# Purpose

The purpose of this document is to collect scientific observations relevant to the possible existence of Scientific Forecast Representation.

The objective is not to explain, classify or interpret the observations.

No scientific properties shall be derived.

No scientific entities shall be introduced.

No architectural conclusions shall be made.

Only observable scientific phenomena shall be collected.

Every observation shall remain independent of implementation, domain knowledge and previously proposed scientific hypotheses.

---

# Scientific Observation Principles

Observation Collection constitutes the first evidence-producing phase of the capability derivation.

Every collected observation shall satisfy the following principles.

---

## SOP-014-001

Observations shall describe only observable scientific phenomena.

No explanation shall be embedded within an observation.

---

## SOP-014-002

Observations shall remain independent of implementation.

Algorithms, artificial intelligence, optimisation methods and prediction techniques shall not influence observation collection.

---

## SOP-014-003

Observations shall remain independent of architectural assumptions.

No observation shall assume the existence of Forecast Representation.

---

## SOP-014-004

Observations shall remain reproducible.

Independent investigators shall be capable of recognising the same observation from identical scientific evidence.

---

## SOP-014-005

Observations shall remain scientifically neutral.

Collected observations shall neither support nor reject the current Methodological Working Hypothesis.

---

# Observation Scope

The present observation collection investigates scientific phenomena occurring whenever future scientific developments are considered.

Only phenomena directly observable from previously validated scientific capability layers shall be collected.

The collection intentionally excludes:

- implementation-specific prediction,
- statistical forecasting,
- probabilistic estimation,
- optimisation,
- decision making,
- planning,
- artificial intelligence,
- machine learning.
