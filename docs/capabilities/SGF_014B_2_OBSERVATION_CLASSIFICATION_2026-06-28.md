# Purpose

The purpose of this document is to classify the scientific observations collected during SGF-014B.1.

Classification does not interpret observations.

Classification does not derive scientific properties.

Classification does not introduce scientific entities.

Classification only determines which observations describe equivalent categories of scientific phenomena.

The objective is to prepare systematic observation grouping during the following derivation phase.

---

# Scientific Classification Principles

Observation Classification shall satisfy the following principles.

---

## SCP-014-001

Classification shall preserve the original observation.

No observation may be modified.

---

## SCP-014-002

Classification shall not introduce interpretation.

Scientific meaning shall be derived only in later derivation phases.

---

## SCP-014-003

Every observation shall belong to at least one scientifically justified observation class.

---

## SCP-014-004

Observation classes shall remain mutually distinguishable.

No observation class shall duplicate another class.

---

## SCP-014-005

Classification shall remain independent of implementation.

---

# Classification Consistency Analysis

The complete observation collection was examined for overlap between observation classes.

The investigation identified no contradictory classifications.

Multiple class membership occurs only where one observation legitimately describes more than one scientific aspect.

Such overlap reflects independent scientific characteristics rather than classification inconsistency.

No observation remained unclassified.

No observation required creation of an additional observation class.
