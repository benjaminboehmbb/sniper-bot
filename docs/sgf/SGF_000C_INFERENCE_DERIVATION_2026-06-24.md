# Inference Validation Criteria v1.0

Every inference shall satisfy the following validation criteria before it may be used for subsequent derivations.

## IVC-001 — Traceability

Every inference shall explicitly reference all supporting observations.

## IVC-002 — Logical Sufficiency

The referenced observations shall be sufficient to support the inference.

## IVC-003 — No Hidden Assumptions

No additional assumptions may be introduced without explicit justification.

## IVC-004 — Domain Neutrality

The inference shall remain independent of any application domain.

## IVC-005 — Solution Neutrality

The inference shall not imply or recommend a future architecture or solution.

## IVC-006 — Minimality

The inference shall contain no information beyond what is logically required.

## IVC-007 — Reproducibility

Independent reviewers shall be able to derive the same inference from the same observations.

---

# Conservative Inference Test (CIT)

Every inference shall additionally satisfy the following review question:

'Is this the weakest statement that necessarily follows from the supporting observations?'

If a weaker logically justified inference exists, the stronger inference shall be rejected.
