# Object Necessity Test (ONT)

Purpose

Determine whether introducing Constraint as a scientific object is scientifically necessary.

The assessment shall attempt to explain the validated evidence without introducing a new scientific object.

Only if this is not possible may object introduction be considered scientifically justified.

---

## Scientific Principle

Scientific objects shall not be introduced by definition.

Scientific objects shall only be introduced when the available validated evidence cannot be sufficiently explained without introducing the proposed object.

---

## ONT-001 — Observation Necessity

Question:

Can the accepted observations be sufficiently explained without introducing Constraint as a scientific object?

Status:

REVIEWED

Evidence:

The accepted observations describe a recurring type of statement whose application to candidate entities may produce different observable outcomes.

Decision:

PASS

Reviewer Reasoning:

The observations can be described without naming Constraint, but their recurring structure suggests that they are not adequately represented by Observation, Inference, Problem or Requirement alone.

---

## ONT-002 — Relationship Necessity

Question:

Can the accepted validated relationships be sufficiently explained without introducing Constraint as a scientific object?

Status:

REVIEWED

Evidence:

CA-001 and CA-002 identify validated relationships among accepted characteristics, including a recurring observed statement type and distinguishable observable outcomes across candidate entities.

Decision:

PASS

Reviewer Reasoning:

The accepted relationships describe a coherent pattern that is not itself an Observation, Inference, Problem or Requirement. A distinct scientific object is justified to preserve epistemic separation.

---

## ONT-003 — Explanatory Improvement

Question:

Would introducing Constraint increase explanatory power beyond the observation and relationship level?

Status:

REVIEWED

Evidence:

The accepted observations and relationships indicate a repeatable statement behavior that affects candidate entities without prescribing a unique solution or implementation.

Decision:

PASS

Reviewer Reasoning:

Introducing Constraint improves explanatory clarity by identifying the role of statements that bound or filter candidates without reducing them to requirements or implementation choices.

---

## ONT-004 — Explanatory Completeness

Question:

Would refusing to introduce Constraint leave validated evidence insufficiently explained?

Status:

REVIEWED

Evidence:

Without a distinct object category, the validated evidence must be forced into previously accepted classes, even though it does not function as Observation, Inference, Problem or Requirement.

Decision:

PASS

Reviewer Reasoning:

Refusing to introduce Constraint would reduce epistemic clarity and would obscure the distinct role observed in the accepted evidence.

The available evidence justifies introducing Constraint as a scientific object.
