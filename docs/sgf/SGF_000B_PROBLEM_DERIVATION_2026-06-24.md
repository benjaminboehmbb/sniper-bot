# Purpose

Derive the fundamental problem that motivates the existence of a persistent decision-making structure for the long-term evolution of scientific systems.

The problem shall not be assumed.

It shall emerge from validated observations.

---

# Scientific Question

Which observable characteristics of long-lived scientific systems make a persistent decision-making structure objectively necessary?

---

# Inputs

Inputs accepted by this investigation:

- validated observations
- scientific findings
- engineering findings
- repository evidence
- logical reasoning

No architectural assumptions shall be introduced.

---

# Observation Registry

## O-001

Long-lived scientific systems continuously evolve over time.

---

## O-002

Architectural and methodological decisions accumulate throughout system evolution.

---

## O-003

Earlier decisions influence later decisions.

---

## O-004

Without systematic documentation, scientific knowledge is gradually lost.

---

## O-006

Increasing system complexity increases the difficulty of maintaining scientific consistency.

---

## O-007

Human reasoning capacity remains limited while system complexity and accumulated knowledge continue to increase.

---

## O-008

Scientific system evolution commonly involves decisions made by different individuals and at different points in time.

---

# Observation Validation

The observations shall satisfy the following conditions:

- domain-independent
- reproducible
- logically independent where possible
- directly observable
- free from architectural assumptions

In addition, every observation shall satisfy the following epistemic rule:

An Observation shall describe only directly observable characteristics.
It shall neither explain, evaluate nor prescribe them.

Observations failing these criteria shall not be used for problem derivation.
