# Purpose

This roadmap defines the planned long-term evolution of the Scientific Governance (SCG) standard series.

Its purpose is to establish a coherent governance architecture that incrementally governs all scientific activities, scientific information, scientific decision making, scientific quality assurance, and scientific platform evolution.

This roadmap is informative rather than normative.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Scientific Governance shall evolve incrementally.

Each governance standard shall define one primary governance responsibility.

Every subsequent governance standard shall extend rather than replace previously established governance.

Governance evolution shall preserve scientific consistency, traceability, reproducibility, and long-term maintainability.

# Governance Architecture

The Scientific Governance standards are designed as an incremental governance hierarchy.

Each standard governs one scientific responsibility while depending upon governance guarantees established by preceding standards.

This architecture minimizes coupling while maximizing traceability, modularity, and long-term extensibility.

---

# Architectural Principles

The Scientific Governance architecture shall satisfy the following principles.

- Domain Independence
- Scientific Integrity
- Governance before Implementation
- Evidence before Conclusions
- Quality before Quantity
- Reproducibility by Design
- Traceability by Design
- Minimal Architecture
- Long-Term Maintainability
- Incremental Evolution

---

# Dependency Principles

Higher-level governance standards may depend upon lower-level governance standards.

Lower-level governance standards shall never depend upon higher-level governance standards.

Dependency relationships shall remain acyclic.

Scientific Governance shall evolve monotonically without invalidating previously released governance standards.

---

# Evolution Principles

New governance standards shall only be introduced when additional governance responsibility is scientifically justified.

Existing standards should remain stable after release.

Major architectural restructuring shall require explicit governance review.

Backward compatibility shall remain the preferred evolution strategy.
